Kitchen

Refrigerator & Freezer

  • Both the water and ice dispensers work on this refrigerator.
  • Sometimes the ice can get a little stuck in its bin — you may hear the dispenser whirring but no ice comes out.
  • If that happens, open the freezer — the ice bin is stored up top.
  • Grab the bin and give it a little jiggle to knock the ice loose, and the dispenser should work perfectly after that.

Stove Top

  • The front right burner is marked — do not use. The igniter has always been finicky on that one and has been repaired many times without success.
  • All other five burners work great — use them freely.
  • If you truly need all six burners, you can turn the front right on and light it with a match if you're comfortable doing so.
  • After the stove has been cleaned, the small caps on top of the burners can shift out of position and a burner may struggle to light.
  • If a burner won't ignite, give the center cap a little jiggle to make sure it's properly seated — it should light right up.

Dining Room

Sonos Sound System

  • There are four Sonos units — one next to the dining room table, one on the TV room bookshelf, one on the table by the front door, and one on the downstairs office bookshelf.
  • The two by the front door and office are portable — take them out to the pool or backyard whenever you like.
  • Please plug them back in when you're done so they're charged and ready for the next guests.
  • Open any music app (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.), tap the AirPlay icon, and you'll see all four rooms listed.
  • Connect to any combination of Dining Room, Family Room, Living Room, and Office.

TV Room

Gas Fireplace

  • To light, turn the valve on the fireplace about a half turn counterclockwise to open the gas.
  • Then use the long lighter (sitting on the hearth) to ignite — it'll make a big whoosh sound, but that's completely normal.
  • The fireplace puts out a lot of heat and really warms up the room nicely.
  • To shut it off, turn the valve clockwise until it's snug — the flame will go out automatically.
  • The coals may keep glowing for a little while after you turn it off — that's normal. As long as the valve is fully closed, it's off.

Gas Fire Pit

  • On the end closest to the pool, open the panel and make sure the gas bottle valve is open.
  • On the side closest to the couch, find the knob and ignition button.
  • Turn the knob about a quarter turn, then press and hold the ignition button — keep holding the knob for a couple seconds until the flame catches.

Pool

  • Start at the valve panel behind the bush near the hot tub — make sure all handles point toward Pool Return and Pool Suction so water flows through the pool.
  • Follow the wall to the cabana where the heater and pump are located.
  • On the pump control panel, press button 2 to increase pump speed to heating mode (button 1 is just the daily filtration setting).
  • On the heater, select Pool, then use the up/down arrows to dial in your desired temperature.
  • Depending on outside temp, it may take a couple hours to heat up — best to start it first thing in the morning on days you plan to swim.

Hot Tub / Spa

  • At the valve panel (back corner near the hot tub), turn the handles to Spa Return and Spa Suction.
  • In the cabana, press button 3 on the pump control — this runs the spa automatically for 2 hours, then shuts off. Just press it again if you need more time.
  • On the heater, select Spa and set your temperature. It's preset to 102°F — a nice comfortable soak.
  • When you're done, come back to the heater and press Spa again to turn off the heat.
  • Before leaving, turn the valves back to Pool Return & Pool Suction so the pool returns to its normal filtration cycle and stays clean.

Outdoor Lighting

  • Look for the green power outlet box at the edge of the deck.
  • Flip the switch on the box to turn on the Italian festival string lights strung around the backyard.
  • Great for evening ambiance — enjoy!

In Case of Emergency

Gas, Water & Electrical

Evacuation

🔥 Fire

  • Exit immediately — don't stop for belongings. Close doors behind you to slow the spread.
  • Use the front door (Barry Ave) or back gate as primary exits.
  • Call 911 once you are safely outside. Do not re-enter.
  • Nearest cross street: Barry Ave & Sepulveda Blvd. Meet there to account for everyone.

🌎 Earthquake

  • During shaking: Drop, cover, and hold on — get under a sturdy table or against an interior wall, away from windows.
  • After shaking stops: Check for injuries. If you smell gas, leave doors open and evacuate immediately — do not use light switches or open flames.
  • Expect aftershocks. Stay outside in an open area away from buildings and power lines.
  • Call 911 for emergencies; text if voice calls are congested.

⚠️ General

  • Emergency services: 911
  • Nearest hospital: Cedars-Sinai Medical Center (~10 min, 8700 Beverly Blvd)
  • If directed to evacuate by authorities, take Sepulveda Blvd north toward the 405 as primary route.
